Alicia Scardetta

Alicia Scardetta is a fiber artist born in Brooklyn, South Texas. She brings a fresh approach to tapestry weaving that highlights the warp rather than hiding it, a technique and the pops of color texture that come with it that bring her work to the edge of the contemporary. Alicia was introduced to weaving through a studio internship at the Textile Arts Center, and further developed her skills in tapestry at the Haystack School of Crafts and Penland School of Crafts. Rooted in experimentation, Alicia's signature style features a bold color palette and innovative techniques honed over a decade of weaving experience.

While studying fine art drawing at the Pratt Institute, Alicia found herself longing to take the line off the page and turn it into a physical, tactile structure, and the fibers and threads felt like the most intuitive way to interpret the line. most of Alicia's fiber work is woven on a frame loom, and she's interested in manipulating the many variables involved in weaving. Exploring what can be produced when warp and weft are challenged. Using vibrant colors, woven attachments, and negative space, each piece achieves an intriguing quality within the historical context of weaving and tapestry.
