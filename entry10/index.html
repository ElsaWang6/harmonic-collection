“From a single thread, we will all be connected.”

Rania Hassan

Rania Hassan is an American composite media artist. She works in site-specific installations that use weaving to translate our connections to time, place and environment into tangible sculptural presentations. The core of her work centers around five main themes: community, commensurability, identity, time and memory.

Born into a Lebanese family with a strong tradition of handicrafts, Rania Hassan started out as an oil painter, but by chance she combined painting with a childhood memory (knitting) to create her current way of working.

Our memories are subtly altered each time they are brought back. But looking back, we can see things more clearly. Rania Hassan is a seamstress who puts these memory fragments back together.

Each of our memories, our experiences, are so interconnected and overlapping that sometimes we only remember the beginning and the end of an event, leaving the in-between process feeling as hazy as a cloud. And she weaves them together again, over and over again, as a way of demonstrating that memories are not isolated, that they exist because the past and present are indelibly intertwined and participate in their eternal cycle of creation, deconstruction and regeneration.
